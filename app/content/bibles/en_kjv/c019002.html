2

1 Why do the heathen rage and the people imagine a vain thing 2 The kings of the earth set themselves, and the rulers take counsel together and against his anointed 3 Let us break their bands asunder and cast away their cords 4 He that sitteth in the heavens shall laugh the Lord shall have them in derision 5 Then shall he speak unto them in his wrath and vex them in his sore displeasure 6 Yet have I set my king upon my holy hill of Zion 7 I will declare the decree hath said my Son this day have I begotten 8 Ask of me, and I shall give the heathen thine inheritance and the uttermost parts of the earth thy possession 9 Thou shalt break them with a rod of iron thou shalt dash them in pieces like a potter' vessel 10 Be wise now therefore, O ye kings be instructed ye judges of the earth 11 Serve with fear and rejoice with trembling 12 Kiss the Son lest he be angry and ye perish the way when his wrath is kindled but a little Blessed all they that put their trust
