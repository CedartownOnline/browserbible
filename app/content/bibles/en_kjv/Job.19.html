Job 20

19

1 Then Job answered and said 2 How long will ye vex my soul and break me in pieces with words 3 These ten times have ye reproached me: ye are not ashamed ye make yourselves strange 4 And be it indeed I have erred mine error remaineth 5 If indeed ye will magnify against me, and plead against me my reproach 6 Know now that God hath overthrown me, and hath compassed me with his net 7 Behold, I cry out of wrong but I am not heard I cry aloud no judgment 8 He hath fenced up my way that I cannot pass and he hath set darkness in my paths 9 He hath stripped me of my glory and taken the crown my head 10 He hath destroyed me on every side and I am gone and mine hope hath he removed like a tree 11 He hath also kindled his wrath against me, and he counteth his enemies 12 His troops come together and raise up their way against me, and encamp round about my tabernacle 13 He hath put my brethren far from me, and mine acquaintance are verily estranged 14 My kinsfolk have failed and my familiar friends have forgotten 15 They that dwell in mine house and my maids count me for a stranger I am an alien in their sight 16 I called my servant no answer I intreated him with my mouth 17 My breath is strange to my wife though I intreated for the children' of mine own body 18 Yea, young children despised me; I arose and they spake 19 All my inward friends abhorred me: and they whom I loved are turned 20 My bone cleaveth to my skin and to my flesh and I am escaped with the skin of my teeth 21 Have pity upon me, have pity upon me, O ye my friends for the hand of God hath touched 22 Why do ye persecute me as God and are not satisfied with my flesh 23 Oh that my words were now written oh that they were printed in a book 24 That they were graven with an iron pen and lead in the rock for ever 25 For I know my redeemer liveth he shall stand at the latter upon the earth 26 after my skin destroy yet in my flesh shall I see God 27 Whom I shall see for myself, and mine eyes shall behold and not another my reins be consumed within 28 But ye should say Why persecute we him, seeing the root of the matter is found 29 Be ye afraid of the sword for wrath the punishments of the sword that ye may know a judgment
