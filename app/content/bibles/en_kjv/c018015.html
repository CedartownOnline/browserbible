Job 16

15

1 Then answered Eliphaz the Temanite and said 2 Should a wise man utter vain knowledge and fill his belly with the east wind 3 Should he reason with unprofitable talk or with speeches wherewith he can do no good 4 Yea, thou castest off fear and restrainest prayer before God 5 For thy mouth uttereth thine iniquity and thou choosest the tongue of the crafty 6 Thine own mouth condemneth thee, and not I: yea, thine own lips testify 7 thou the first man was born or wast thou made before the hills 8 Hast thou heard the secret of God and dost thou restrain wisdom 9 What knowest thou, that we know understandest 10 both the grayheaded and very aged men, much elder than thy father 11 the consolations of God small with thee? is there any secret thing 12 Why doth thine heart carry thee away and what do thy eyes wink at 13 That thou turnest thy spirit against God words go out of thy mouth 14 man that he should be clean born of a woman that he should be righteous 15 Behold, he putteth no trust in his saints yea, the heavens are not clean in his sight 16 How much more abominable and filthy man which drinketh iniquity like water 17 I will shew thee, hear I have seen I will declare 18 Which wise men have told from their fathers and have not hid 19 Unto whom alone the earth was given and no stranger passed among 20 The wicked man travaileth days and the number of years is hidden to the oppressor 21 A dreadful sound in his ears in prosperity the destroyer shall come 22 He believeth not that he shall return out of darkness and he is waited for of the sword 23 He wandereth abroad for bread he knoweth that the day of darkness is ready at his hand 24 Trouble and anguish shall make him afraid they shall prevail against him, as a king ready to the battle 25 For he stretcheth out his hand against God and strengtheneth himself against the Almighty 26 He runneth neck upon the thick bosses of his bucklers 27 Because he covereth his face with his fatness and maketh collops of fat flanks 28 And he dwelleth in desolate cities in houses which no man inhabiteth which are ready to become heaps 29 He shall not be rich neither shall his substance continue neither shall he prolong the perfection thereof upon the earth 30 He shall not depart out of darkness the flame shall dry up his branches and by the breath of his mouth shall he go away 31 Let not him that is deceived trust in vanity for vanity shall be his recompence 32 It shall be accomplished before his time and his branch shall not be green 33 He shall shake off his unripe grape as the vine and shall cast off his flower as the olive 34 For the congregation of hypocrites desolate and fire shall consume the tabernacles of bribery 35 They conceive mischief and bring forth vanity and their belly prepareth deceit
