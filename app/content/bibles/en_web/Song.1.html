1 The Song of songs, which is Solomon’s. 2 Let him kiss me with the kisses of his mouth; 3 Your oils have a pleasing fragrance. 4 Take me away with you. 5 I am dark, but lovely, 6 Don’t stare at me because I am dark, 7 Tell me, you whom my soul loves, 8 If you don’t know, most beautiful among women, 9 I have compared you, my love, 10 Your cheeks are beautiful with earrings, 11 We will make you earrings of gold, 12 While the king sat at his table, 13 My beloved is to me a sachet of myrrh, 14 My beloved is to me a cluster of henna blossoms 15 Behold,+“Behold,” from “הִנֵּה”, means look at, take notice, observe, see, or gaze at. It is often used as an interjection. you are beautiful, my love. 16 Behold, you are beautiful, my beloved, yes, pleasant; 17 The beams of our house are cedars.
