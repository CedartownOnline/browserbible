1 Thus saith to his anointed to Cyrus whose right hand I have holden to subdue nations before him; and I will loose the loins of kings to open before him the two leaved gates and the gates shall not be shut 2 I will go before thee, and make the crooked places straight I will break in pieces the gates of brass and cut in sunder the bars of iron 3 And I will give thee the treasures of darkness and hidden riches of secret places that thou mayest know which call by thy name the God of Israel 4 For Jacob my servant' sake, and Israel mine elect I have even called thee by thy name I have surnamed thee, though thou hast not known 5 no God beside me: I girded thee, though thou hast not known 6 That they may know from the rising of the sun and from the west none beside 7 I form the light and create darkness I make peace and create evil do 8 Drop down ye heavens from above and let the skies pour down righteousness let the earth open and let them bring forth salvation and let righteousness spring up together have created 9 Woe unto him that striveth with his Maker the potsherd with the potsherds of the earth Shall the clay say to him that fashioneth it, What makest thou? or thy work He hath no hands 10 Woe unto him that saith father What begettest thou? or to the woman What hast thou brought forth 11 Thus saith the Holy One of Israel and his Maker Ask me of things to come concerning my sons and concerning the work of my hands command 12 I have made the earth and created man my hands have stretched out the heavens and all their host have I commanded 13 I have raised him up in righteousness and I will direct all his ways he shall build my city and he shall let go my captives not for price nor reward saith of hosts 14 Thus saith The labour of Egypt and merchandise of Ethiopia and of the Sabeans men of stature shall come over unto thee, and they shall be thine: they shall come after thee; in chains they shall come over and they shall fall down unto thee, they shall make supplication Surely God no God 15 Verily a God that hidest thyself, O God of Israel the Saviour 16 They shall be ashamed and also confounded all of them: they shall go to confusion together makers of idols 17 Israel shall be saved with an everlasting salvation ye shall not be ashamed nor confounded world without end 18 For thus saith that created the heavens God himself that formed the earth and made it; he hath established it, he created it not in vain he formed it to be inhabited 19 I have not spoken in secret in a dark place of the earth I said not unto the seed of Jacob Seek ye me in vain speak righteousness I declare things that are right 20 Assemble yourselves and come draw near together escaped of the nations they have no knowledge that set up the wood of their graven image and pray unto a god cannot save 21 Tell near yea, let them take counsel together who hath declared this from ancient time hath told no God else beside me; a just God and a Saviour none beside 22 Look unto me, and be ye saved all the ends of the earth God 23 I have sworn by myself, the word is gone out of my mouth righteousness and shall not return That unto me every knee shall bow every tongue shall swear 24 say have I righteousness and strength come and all that are incensed against him shall be ashamed 25 shall all the seed of Israel be justified and shall glory
