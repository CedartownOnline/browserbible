17

1 And Elijah the Tishbite of the inhabitants of Gilead said unto Ahab God of Israel liveth before whom I stand there shall not be dew nor rain these years but according to my word 2 And the word came unto him, saying 3 Get thee hence, and turn thee eastward and hide thyself by the brook Cherith before Jordan 4 thou shalt drink of the brook and I have commanded the ravens to feed 5 So he went and did according unto the word for he went and dwelt by the brook Cherith before Jordan 6 And the ravens brought him bread and flesh in the morning and bread and flesh in the evening and he drank of the brook 7 And it came to pass after a while that the brook dried up because there had been no rain in the land 8 And the word came unto him, saying 9 Arise get thee to Zarephath to Zidon and dwell there: behold, I have commanded a widow woman there to sustain 10 So he arose and went to Zarephath And when he came to the gate of the city behold, the widow woman there gathering of sticks and he called to her, and said Fetch me, I pray thee, a little water in a vessel that I may drink 11 And as she was going to fetch he called to her, and said Bring me, I pray thee, a morsel of bread in thine hand 12 And she said thy God liveth I have not a cake but an handful of meal in a barrel and a little oil in a cruse gathering two sticks that I may go in and dress it for me and my son that we may eat it, and die 13 And Elijah said unto her, Fear not; go do as thou hast said but make me thereof a little cake first and bring unto me, and after make for thee and for thy son 14 For thus saith God of Israel The barrel of meal shall not waste neither shall the cruse of oil fail until the day sendeth rain upon the earth 15 And she went and did according to the saying of Elijah and she, and he, and her house did eat days 16 the barrel of meal wasted not, neither did the cruse of oil fail according to the word which he spake by Elijah 17 And it came to pass after these things the son of the woman the mistress of the house fell sick and his sickness was so sore that there was no breath left 18 And she said unto Elijah What have I to do with thee, O thou man of God art thou come unto me to call my sin to remembrance and to slay my son 19 And he said unto her, Give me thy son And he took him out of her bosom and carried him up into a loft where he abode and laid him upon his own bed 20 And he cried and said my God hast thou also brought evil upon the widow with whom I sojourn by slaying her son 21 And he stretched himself upon the child three times and cried and said my God I pray thee, let this child' soul come into him again 22 heard the voice of Elijah and the soul of the child came into him again and he revived 23 And Elijah took the child and brought him down out of the chamber into the house and delivered him unto his mother and Elijah said See thy son liveth 24 And the woman said to Elijah Now by this I know a man of God that the word in thy mouth truth
