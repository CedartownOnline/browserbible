Malachi

1

1 The burden of the word to Israel by Malachi 2 I have loved you, saith Yet ye say Wherein hast thou loved not Esau Jacob' brother saith yet I loved Jacob 3 And I hated Esau and laid his mountains and his heritage waste for the dragons of the wilderness 4 Whereas Edom saith We are impoverished but we will return and build the desolate places thus saith of hosts They shall build but I will throw down and they shall call them, The border of wickedness and, The people hath indignation for ever 5 And your eyes shall see and ye shall say will be magnified from the border of Israel 6 A son honoureth father and a servant his master a father mine honour a master my fear saith of hosts unto you, O priests that despise my name And ye say Wherein have we despised thy name 7 Ye offer polluted bread upon mine altar and ye say Wherein have we polluted thee? In that ye say The table contemptible 8 And if ye offer the blind for sacrifice not evil and if ye offer the lame and sick not evil offer it now unto thy governor will he be pleased with thee, or accept thy person saith of hosts 9 And now, I pray you, beseech God that he will be gracious unto us: this hath been by your means will he regard your persons saith of hosts 10 even among you that would shut the doors neither do ye kindle on mine altar for nought I have no pleasure in you, saith of hosts neither will I accept an offering at your hand 11 For from the rising of the sun even unto the going down of the same my name great among the Gentiles and in every place incense offered unto my name and a pure offering for my name great among the heathen saith of hosts 12 But ye have profaned it, in that ye say The table polluted and the fruit his meat contemptible 13 Ye said also, Behold, what a weariness and ye have snuffed at it, saith of hosts and ye brought torn and the lame and the sick thus ye brought an offering should I accept this of your hand saith 14 But cursed the deceiver which hath in his flock a male and voweth and sacrificeth unto the Lord a corrupt thing a great King saith of hosts and my name dreadful among the heathen
