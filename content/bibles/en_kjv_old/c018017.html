17

1 My breath is corrupt my days are extinct the graves 2 not mockers with me? and doth not mine eye continue in their provocation 3 Lay down now, put me in a surety will strike hands 4 For thou hast hid their heart from understanding therefore shalt thou not exalt 5 He that speaketh flattery friends even the eyes of his children shall fail 6 He hath made me also a byword of the people and aforetime I was as a tabret 7 Mine eye also is dim by reason of sorrow and all my members as a shadow 8 Upright shall be astonied at this, and the innocent shall stir up himself against the hypocrite 9 The righteous also shall hold on his way and he that hath clean hands shall be stronger and stronger 10 But as for you all, do ye return and come now for I cannot find wise 11 My days are past my purposes are broken off the thoughts of my heart 12 They change the night into day the light short because of darkness 13 If I wait the grave mine house I have made my bed in the darkness 14 I have said to corruption my father to the worm my mother and my sister 15 now my hope as for my hope who shall see 16 They shall go down to the bars of the pit rest together in the dust
