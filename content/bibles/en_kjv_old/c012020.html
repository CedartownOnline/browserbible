1 In those days was Hezekiah sick unto death And the prophet Isaiah the son of Amoz came to him, and said unto him, Thus saith Set thine house in order for thou shalt die and not live 2 Then he turned his face to the wall and prayed saying 3 I beseech remember now how I have walked before thee in truth and with a perfect heart and have done good in thy sight And Hezekiah wept sore 4 And it came to pass, afore Isaiah was gone out into the middle court that the word came to him, saying 5 Turn again and tell Hezekiah the captain of my people Thus saith the God of David thy father I have heard thy prayer I have seen thy tears behold, I will heal thee: on the third day thou shalt go up unto the house 6 And I will add unto thy days fifteen years and I will deliver thee and this city out of the hand of the king of Assyria and I will defend this city for mine own sake, and for my servant David' 7 And Isaiah said Take a lump of figs And they took and laid on the boil and he recovered 8 And Hezekiah said unto Isaiah the sign will heal me, and that I shall go up into the house the third day 9 And Isaiah said This sign will do the thing that he hath spoken shall the shadow go forward ten degrees or go back ten degrees 10 And Hezekiah answered It is a light thing for the shadow to go down ten degrees nay, but let the shadow return backward ten degrees 11 And Isaiah the prophet cried and he brought the shadow ten degrees backward by which it had gone down in the dial of Ahaz 12 At that time Berodachbaladan the son of Baladan king of Babylon sent letters and a present unto Hezekiah for he had heard that Hezekiah had been sick 13 And Hezekiah hearkened unto them, and shewed them all the house of his precious things the silver and the gold and the spices and the precious ointment the house of his armour and all that was found in his treasures there was nothing in his house nor in all his dominion that Hezekiah shewed 14 Then came Isaiah the prophet unto king Hezekiah and said unto him, What said these men and from whence came they unto thee? And Hezekiah said They are come from a far country from Babylon 15 And he said What have they seen in thine house And Hezekiah answered in mine house have they seen there is nothing among my treasures that I have not shewed 16 And Isaiah said unto Hezekiah Hear the word 17 Behold, the days come in thine house and that which thy fathers have laid up in store unto this day shall be carried into Babylon nothing shall be left saith 18 And of thy sons that shall issue from thee, which thou shalt beget shall they take away and they shall be eunuchs in the palace of the king of Babylon 19 Then said Hezekiah unto Isaiah Good the word which thou hast spoken And he said if peace and truth be in my days 20 And the rest of the acts of Hezekiah and all his might and how he made a pool and a conduit and brought water into the city they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Judah 21 And Hezekiah slept with his fathers and Manasseh his son reigned
