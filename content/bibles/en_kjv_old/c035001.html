Habakkuk

1

1 The burden which Habakkuk the prophet did see 2 how long shall I cry and thou wilt not hear cry out violence and thou wilt not save 3 Why dost thou shew me iniquity to behold grievance for spoiling and violence raise up strife and contention 4 Therefore the law is slacked and judgment doth never go forth for the wicked doth compass about the righteous therefore wrong judgment proceedeth 5 Behold ye among the heathen and regard and wonder marvellously will work a work in your days ye will not believe though it be told 6 For, lo, I raise up the Chaldeans bitter and hasty nation which shall march through the breadth of the land to possess the dwellingplaces 7 terrible and dreadful their judgment and their dignity shall proceed 8 Their horses also are swifter than the leopards and are more fierce than the evening wolves and their horsemen shall spread themselves, and their horsemen shall come from far they shall fly as the eagle hasteth to eat 9 They shall come all for violence their faces shall sup up the east wind and they shall gather the captivity as the sand 10 And they shall scoff at the kings and the princes shall be a scorn unto them: they shall deride every strong hold for they shall heap dust and take 11 mind change and he shall pass over and offend this his power unto his god 12 thou not from everlasting my God mine Holy One we shall not die thou hast ordained them for judgment and, O mighty God thou hast established them for correction 13 of purer eyes than to behold evil and canst not look on iniquity wherefore lookest thou upon them that deal treacherously holdest thy tongue when the wicked devoureth more righteous 14 And makest men as the fishes of the sea as the creeping things no ruler 15 They take up all of them with the angle they catch them in their net and gather them in their drag therefore they rejoice and are glad 16 Therefore they sacrifice unto their net and burn incense unto their drag because by them their portion fat and their meat plenteous 17 Shall they therefore empty their net and not spare continually to slay the nations
