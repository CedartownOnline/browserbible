23

1 Then Job answered and said 2 Even to day my complaint bitter my stroke is heavier than my groaning 3 Oh that I knew where I might find I might come to his seat 4 I would order cause before him, and fill my mouth with arguments 5 I would know the words he would answer me, and understand what he would say 6 Will he plead against great power No; but he would put 7 There the righteous might dispute with him; so should I be delivered for ever from my judge 8 Behold, I go forward and backward but I cannot perceive 9 On the left hand where he doth work but I cannot behold he hideth himself on the right hand that I cannot see 10 But he knoweth the way that I take he hath tried me, I shall come forth as gold 11 My foot hath held his steps his way have I kept and not declined 12 Neither have I gone back from the commandment of his lips I have esteemed the words of his mouth more than my necessary 13 in one and who can turn his soul desireth he doeth 14 For he performeth appointed for me: and many such 15 Therefore am I troubled at his presence when I consider I am afraid 16 For God maketh my heart soft and the Almighty troubleth 17 Because I was not cut off before the darkness hath he covered the darkness from my face
