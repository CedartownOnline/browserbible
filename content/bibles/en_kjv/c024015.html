15

1 Then said unto me, Though Moses and Samuel stood before my mind toward this people out of my sight and let them go forth 2 And it shall come to pass, if they say unto thee, Whither shall we go forth then thou shalt tell them, Thus saith for death to death for the sword to the sword for the famine to the famine for the captivity to the captivity 3 And I will appoint over them four kinds saith the sword to slay and the dogs to tear and the fowls of the heaven and the beasts of the earth to devour and destroy 4 And I will cause them to be removed into all kingdoms of the earth because of Manasseh the son of Hezekiah king of Judah which he did in Jerusalem 5 For who shall have pity upon thee, O Jerusalem or who shall bemoan thee? or who shall go aside to ask how thou doest 6 Thou hast forsaken me, saith thou art gone backward therefore will I stretch out my hand against thee, and destroy thee; I am weary with repenting 7 And I will fan them with a fan in the gates of the land I will bereave of children, I will destroy my people they return not from their ways 8 Their widows are increased to me above the sand of the seas I have brought upon them against the mother of the young men a spoiler at noonday to fall upon it suddenly and terrors upon the city 9 She that hath borne seven languisheth she hath given up the ghost her sun is gone yet day she hath been ashamed and confounded and the residue of them will I deliver to the sword before their enemies saith 10 Woe is me, my mother that thou hast borne me a man of strife and a man of contention to the whole earth I have neither lent on usury nor men have lent to me on usury every one of them doth curse 11 said Verily it shall be well with thy remnant verily I will cause the enemy to entreat in the time of evil and in the time of affliction 12 Shall iron break the northern iron and the steel 13 Thy substance and thy treasures will I give to the spoil without price for all thy sins even in all thy borders 14 to pass with thine enemies into a land thou knowest not: for a fire is kindled in mine anger shall burn 15 thou knowest remember me, and visit me, and revenge me of my persecutors take me not away in thy longsuffering know that for thy sake I have suffered rebuke 16 Thy words were found and I did eat them; and thy word was unto me the joy and rejoicing of mine heart for I am called by thy name God of hosts 17 I sat not in the assembly of the mockers nor rejoiced I sat alone because of thy hand for thou hast filled me with indignation 18 Why is my pain perpetual and my wound incurable refuseth to be healed wilt thou be altogether unto me as a liar waters fail 19 Therefore thus saith If thou return then will I bring thee again thou shalt stand before me: and if thou take forth the precious from the vile thou shalt be as my mouth let them return unto thee; but return 20 And I will make thee unto this people a fenced brasen wall and they shall fight against thee, but they shall not prevail with thee to save thee and to deliver thee, saith 21 And I will deliver thee out of the hand of the wicked and I will redeem thee out of the hand of the terrible
