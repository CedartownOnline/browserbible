23

1 And Balaam said unto Balak Build me here seven altars and prepare me here seven oxen and seven rams 2 And Balak did as Balaam had spoken and Balak and Balaam offered altar a bullock and a ram 3 And Balaam said unto Balak Stand by thy burnt offering and I will go will come to meet me: and whatsoever he sheweth me I will tell thee. And he went to an high place 4 And God met Balaam and he said unto him, I have prepared seven altars and I have offered altar a bullock and a ram 5 put a word in Balaam' mouth and said Return unto Balak and thus thou shalt speak 6 And he returned unto him, and, lo, he stood by his burnt sacrifice he, and all the princes of Moab 7 And he took up his parable and said Balak the king of Moab hath brought me from Aram out of the mountains of the east Come curse me Jacob and come defy Israel 8 How shall I curse whom God hath not cursed or how shall I defy hath not defied 9 For from the top of the rocks I see him, and from the hills I behold him: lo, the people shall dwell alone and shall not be reckoned among the nations 10 Who can count the dust of Jacob and the number of the fourth of Israel Let me die the death of the righteous and let my last 11 And Balak said unto Balaam What hast thou done unto me? I took thee to curse mine enemies and, behold, thou hast blessed altogether 12 And he answered and said Must I not take heed to speak hath put in my mouth 13 And Balak said unto him, Come I pray thee, with me unto another place from whence thou mayest see them: thou shalt see but the utmost part of them, and shalt not see them all: and curse 14 And he brought him into the field of Zophim to the top of Pisgah and built seven altars and offered a bullock and a ram altar 15 And he said unto Balak Stand here by thy burnt offering while I meet yonder 16 met Balaam and put a word in his mouth and said Go again unto Balak and say 17 And when he came to him, behold, he stood by his burnt offering and the princes of Moab with him. And Balak said spoken 18 And he took up his parable and said Rise up Balak and hear hearken unto me, thou son of Zippor 19 God not a man that he should lie neither the son of man that he should repent hath he said and shall he not do or hath he spoken and shall he not make it good 20 Behold, I have received to bless and he hath blessed and I cannot reverse 21 He hath not beheld iniquity in Jacob neither hath he seen perverseness in Israel his God with him, and the shout of a king 22 God brought them out of Egypt he hath as it were the strength of an unicorn 23 no enchantment against Jacob any divination against Israel according to this time it shall be said of Jacob and of Israel What hath God wrought 24 Behold, the people shall rise up as a great lion and lift up himself as a young lion he shall not lie down until he eat the prey and drink the blood of the slain 25 And Balak said unto Balaam Neither curse them at all nor bless them at all 26 But Balaam answered and said unto Balak Told not I thee, saying speaketh that I must do 27 And Balak said unto Balaam Come I pray thee, I will bring thee unto another place peradventure it will please God that thou mayest curse 28 And Balak brought Balaam unto the top of Peor that looketh toward Jeshimon 29 And Balaam said unto Balak Build me here seven altars and prepare me here seven bullocks and seven rams 30 And Balak did as Balaam had said and offered a bullock and a ram altar
