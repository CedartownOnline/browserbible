Deuteronomy 15 (KJV)

Deut 16

15

1 At the end seven years thou shalt make a release 2 the manner of the release Every creditor that lendeth unto his neighbour shall release he shall not exact of his neighbour or of his brother because it is called release 3 Of a foreigner thou mayest exact which is thine with thy brother thine hand shall release 4 Save when there shall be no poor shall greatly bless thee in the land thy God giveth an inheritance to possess 5 Only if thou carefully hearken unto the voice thy God to observe to do all these commandments which I command thee this day 6 thy God blesseth thee, as he promised thee: and thou shalt lend unto many nations but thou shalt not borrow and thou shalt reign over many nations but they shall not reign 7 If there be among you a poor man of one of thy brethren within any of thy gates in thy land thy God giveth thee, thou shalt not harden thine heart nor shut thine hand from thy poor brother 8 But thou shalt open thine hand wide unto him, and shalt surely lend him sufficient for his need which he wanteth 9 Beware that there be not a thought in thy wicked heart saying The seventh year the year of release is at hand and thine eye be evil against thy poor brother and thou givest him nought; and he cry against thee, and it be sin 10 Thou shalt surely give him, and thine heart shall not be grieved when thou givest unto him: because that for this thing thy God shall bless thee in all thy works and in all that thou puttest thine hand 11 For the poor shall never cease out of the land therefore I command thee, saying Thou shalt open thine hand wide unto thy brother to thy poor and to thy needy in thy land 12 if thy brother an Hebrew man or an Hebrew woman be sold unto thee, and serve thee six years then in the seventh year thou shalt let him go free 13 And when thou sendest him out free from thee, thou shalt not let him go away empty 14 Thou shalt furnish him liberally out of thy flock and out of thy floor and out of thy winepress thy God hath blessed thee thou shalt give 15 And thou shalt remember that thou wast a bondman in the land of Egypt thy God redeemed thee: therefore I command thee this thing to day 16 And it shall be, if he say unto thee, I will not go away from thee; because he loveth thee and thine house because he is well 17 Then thou shalt take an aul and thrust through his ear unto the door and he shall be thy servant for ever And also unto thy maidservant thou shalt do 18 It shall not seem hard unto thee when thou sendest him away free from thee; for he hath been worth a double hired servant in serving thee six years thy God shall bless thee in all that thou doest 19 All the firstling males that come of thy herd and of thy flock thou shalt sanctify thy God thou shalt do no work with the firstling of thy bullock nor shear the firstling of thy sheep 20 Thou shalt eat before thy God year by year in the place shall choose thou and thy household 21 blemish lame or blind any ill blemish thou shalt not sacrifice thy God 22 Thou shalt eat it within thy gates the unclean and the clean alike as the roebuck and as the hart 23 Only thou shalt not eat the blood thereof; thou shalt pour it upon the ground as water
