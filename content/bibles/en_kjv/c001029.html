1 Then Jacob went on his journey and came into the land of the people of the east 2 And he looked and behold a well in the field three flocks of sheep lying by it; for out of that well they watered the flocks and a great stone upon the well' mouth 3 And thither were all the flocks gathered and they rolled the stone from the well' mouth and watered the sheep and put the stone again upon the well' mouth in his place 4 And Jacob said unto them, My brethren whence ye? And they said Of Haran 5 And he said unto them, Know ye Laban the son of Nahor And they said We know 6 And he said he well And they said well and, behold, Rachel his daughter cometh with the sheep 7 And he said Lo yet high day time that the cattle should be gathered together water ye the sheep and go feed 8 And they said We cannot until all the flocks be gathered together they roll the stone from the well' mouth then we water the sheep 9 And while he yet spake with them, Rachel came with her father' sheep for she kept them 10 And it came to pass, when Jacob saw Rachel the daughter of Laban his mother' brother and the sheep of Laban his mother' brother that Jacob went near and rolled the stone from the well' mouth and watered the flock of Laban his mother' brother 11 And Jacob kissed Rachel and lifted up his voice and wept 12 And Jacob told Rachel her father' brother Rebekah' son and she ran and told her father 13 And it came to pass, when Laban heard the tidings of Jacob his sister' son that he ran to meet him, and embraced him, and kissed him, and brought him to his house And he told Laban all these things 14 And Laban said to him, Surely my bone and my flesh And he abode with him the space of a month 15 And Laban said unto Jacob my brother shouldest thou therefore serve me for nought tell thy wages 16 And Laban had two daughters the name of the elder Leah and the name of the younger Rachel 17 Leah tender eyed but Rachel was beautiful and well favoured 18 And Jacob loved Rachel and said I will serve thee seven years for Rachel thy younger daughter 19 And Laban said better that I give her to thee, than that I should give her to another man abide 20 And Jacob served seven years for Rachel and they seemed a few days for the love he had 21 And Jacob said
